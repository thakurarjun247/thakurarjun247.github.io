Being the source the model trusts is the new game.

3/
① llms.txt

A clean, plain-text profile at your site root.

LLMs love quotable text — this hands it to them without fighting your HTML.

Who you are, what you do, what you're available for. That's it.

4/
② A connected entity graph (JSON-LD @id)

Don't scatter separate Person / Org / Website blobs.

Give each a stable @id and link them, so engines resolve you as ONE entity — not three strangers with the same name.

5/
③ Real FAQs as FAQPage schema

LLMs extract question→answer pairs beautifully.

Marking up genuine FAQs turns them into ready-made citations.

6/
④ Let the AI crawlers in

Most sites silently block them. I explicitly allow:

GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot, CCBot (Common Crawl — feeds many models)

in robots.txt.

7/
⑤ Get indexed fast

Fresh sitemap.xml + IndexNow → instant ping to Bing & Yandex.

Bing's index feeds ChatGPT and Perplexity. That's the fast lane.

8/
Why it works:

AI answers are sourced from search indexes + Common Crawl.

Content that's structured, quotable & corroborated is what gets cited.

GEO isn't a trick — it's making the truth about you easy for a machine to read.

9/
