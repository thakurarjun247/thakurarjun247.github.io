SEO gets you ranked on Google. GEO — Generative Engine Optimization — gets you *quoted* by ChatGPT, Claude, and Perplexity. As buyers increasingly ask an AI "who can help me build/learn this?", being the source the model trusts is the new game.

Here's what I did to my site (arjunthakur.dev):

1. llms.txt — a clean, plain-text profile at the site root that AI crawlers can lift facts from.

2. A connected entity graph (JSON-LD @id) — so engines resolve Person + Company + Website as ONE entity, not three strangers.

3. Real FAQs marked up as FAQPage — question/answer pairs are ready-made citations for LLMs.

4. robots.txt that explicitly ALLOWS the AI crawlers (GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot, CCBot). Most sites accidentally block them.

5. sitemap + IndexNow to get indexed fast (Bing's index feeds ChatGPT and Perplexity).

The significance: AI answers come from search indexes + Common Crawl. Content that's structured, quotable, and corroborated is what gets cited. GEO isn't a trick — it's making the truth about you easy for a machine to read.
